Goals and Objectives

Following are the goals and objectives of our websites.

GOAL 1:
Improve lifelong healthy eating

Objectives:

  • Increase awareness and knowledge about healthy eating.
  • Increase awareness of food and recipe label reading, enabling people to make better informed choices about the food they consume.
  • Increase awareness of appropriate portion sizes.
  • Increase fruit andvegetable consumption to the recommended 7– 10 servings a day.
  • Improve healthy food choices for children and adolescents at school, after school activities, colleges and universities as well as recreation facilities.
  • Work with schools to enhance health curriculum to address nutrition and increase  nutrition awareness with parents.
  • Improve behavioral counseling skills at all levels of health care delivery.
  • Work with local policymakers to develop and implement guidelines and policies to ensure that foods and beverages available are consistent with existing nutritional guidelines.  Advocate for policy change eg. Trans fat ban, caloric/fat/sodium content listing.
  • Increase food security and improve access and affordability to healthy foods especially fruit and vegetables.  Increase amount of fruit and vegetables to food banks and soup kitchens.

GOAL 2:
Increase lifelong physical activity

Objectives:

  • Increase number of adolescents and adults aware of current physical activity guidelinesand recommendations.
  • Increase the number of workplaces offering employer-sponsored workplace wellness programs eg. subsidized fitness programs, walking breaks.
  • Improve accessibility and affordability of physical activity choices for children and adolescents at school, after school programs/activities, colleges and universities as well as recreation facilities.
  • Work with schools to enhance health curriculum to devote adequate attention to physical activity and increase physical activity benefits awareness with parents.
  • Work with healthcare community, educational, and community partners to raise awareness and improve dissemination of physical activity guidelines and recommendations.  Increase the number of healthcare workers who use effective methods to encourage patients to increase physical activity.
  • Promote and encourage Active Transportation.

GOAL 3:
Increase the awareness of health risks associated with obesity, overweight, physical inactivity and poor nutrition

Objectives:

  • Increase awareness of the costs – personal and financial, related diseases, absenteeism and the socioeconomic and personal factors associated with overweight and obesity.
  • Increase awareness that this epidemic will shorten lifespans and add years of chronic illness to all affected.
  • Work with healthcare community to increase the proportion of health care providers and institutions who discuss health risks associated with overweight and obesity and benefits of healthy eating and active living. Use the “language” of their patients.
  • Foster and develop relationships with local groups/individuals actively engaged with promoting healthy eating and physical activity opportunities across Cape Breton.

GOAL 4:
Decrease sedentary screen time

Objectives:

  • Decrease exposure to television, computer and other recreational screen time to no more
    than two hours a day.
  • Increase awareness and knowledge of recommendations to limit television viewing and other recreational screen time.
  • Work with healthcare community, educators and others to develop efficient ways to counsel individuals, families and other caregivers about limiting television viewing and other recreational screen time (videos, computer, video games), internet to no more than 1-2 hours
    a day.
  • Encourage industry to reduce advertising of high calorie, low nutrient food and beverages
    to children.

GOAL 5:
Utilize program evaluation to assess best practice evidence to prevent overweight and obesity

Objectives:

  • Analyze existing data.
  • Disseminate best practices.
  • Enhance, expand and strengthen surveillance.
  • Implement effective interventions.
  • Work across all age groups, gender, education level, income level, racial/ethnic groups and socioeconomic levels.
